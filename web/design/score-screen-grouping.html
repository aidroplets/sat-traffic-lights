Tradeoffs at a glance

LayoutVisual weightProsCons
0 · Current None — flat Simplest markup. Nothing to maintain. The "messy" feeling you flagged. Sections feel orphaned. Hint text floats.
1 · Bordered subsections (yours) Heaviest — bordered cards Unambiguous grouping. Toggle visibly belongs to Difficulty. Easy to spot the two decisions. Double borders (outer card + inner cards). Can feel boxy / form-y. Adds visual chrome.
2 · Tinted blocks recommended Medium — fill, no border Same grouping clarity as Option 1, no border-on-border issue. Softer rhythm. Reads as "containers" without looking like nested forms. Subtle gray-on-darker-gray contrast can feel washed if the body bg shifts.
3 · Hairline dividers Lightest Minimal chrome. Each section is its own row but no boxy feeling. Settings-page convention. Less obvious that "the toggle belongs to Difficulty" — it could read as its own section.
4 · Two-column desktop Medium Halves the vertical space on desktop. Both decisions visible at once. Best for power use / repeat visits. Tier tiles compress to 2×2 (less dominant). Falls back to stacked on phones — extra responsive complexity.
5 · Rail labels Light Labels never compete with content. Reads as "form rows." Compact. Doesn't render as well on narrow viewports. The label rail eats horizontal space.

My pick: Option 2 — Tinted blocks

Solves the same problem as your idea (visible grouping, toggle belongs to Difficulty) without doubling up borders against the outer card. Reads as "containers" because of the fill, not "form fields." If you want stronger grouping, Option 1 (your idea) is the next step up. If you want lighter, Option 3 (dividers) gets you most of the way there.
